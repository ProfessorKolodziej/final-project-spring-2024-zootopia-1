Family and Habitat

Wolves belong to the Canidae family. Live in forests of North America, the grasslands of Eurasia, and the Arctic tundra.

Appearance and Characteristics

Wolves resemble their relatives—domestic dogs—but wolves are larger, with longer legs and sharper ears. Grey, white, or black fur, which helps them camouflage in different environments. Wolves' eyes are very distinctive, usually deep yellow or pale green, can reflect light at night.

Diet and Hunting Methods

Carnivorous animals, mainly feeding on deer, elk, and other large mammals. Hunt very cohesively, with the whole pack cooperating, using intelligence and strength to capture prey.
