SHEEP

Family and Habitat

Farm sheep are herbivorous animals, typically living in spacious pastures. Pastures provide enough grass for the sheep to graze and roam. In the pasture, sheep often gather in flocks with other sheep, forming close group relationships.

Appearance and Characteristics

Farm sheep usually have soft, fluffy wool, which can be of various colors such as white, black, and brown. They have gentle eyes and flexible ears, appearing very cute. The tails of farm sheep are usually short.

Dietary Habits

Farm sheep are herbivorous animals, primarily eating grass, herbs, and other pasture plants. They use sharp teeth to clip off parts of the grass, which is then digested through their robust stomachs. Farm sheep spend a lot of time grazing in the pasture to maintain health
